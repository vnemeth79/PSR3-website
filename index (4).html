§ 01 — The Framework

Five pillars. One integrated system for the infrastructure society depends on.

Conventional risk practice treats energy, healthcare, defence, production, and IT as separate domains. PSR³ treats them as a single coupled system — because that is how they fail, and that is how they must be defended.

I Energy

When the grid dies, we stay alive.

Permanent energy autonomy for critical facilities. Patented waste-heat recovery combined with AI-optimised renewable management — not hours on diesel, but continuous island-mode operation.

  • • US provisional patent (heat recovery + solar hybrid)
  • • Solar PdM: −47% unplanned downtime, +20–25% asset lifespan
  • • AI grid monitoring & predictive infrastructure defence
II Healthcare

Keeping healthcare operational under stress.

Hospitals are energy-dependent, data-intensive, physically vulnerable critical infrastructure. PSR³ treats them as such — covering device fleet integrity, clinical workflow resilience, and regulatory continuity.

  • • AgentQAMed — predictive medical device QA (EU MDR, AI Act)
  • • National device-fleet monitoring: MRI, ventilators, surgical robotics
  • • Continuous compliance automation & audit trail
III Defence

Civilian infrastructure. Military-grade protection.

Physical and cyber defence for critical civilian assets. AI-powered drone detection, containerised sovereign manufacturing capability, and a proprietary distributed data architecture that survives active attack.

  • • Counter-drone detection, classification, automated escalation
  • • Containerised drone-manufacturing cells (co-production ready)
  • • Decentralised data architecture — TRL 6, zero data loss under attack
IV Production

When borders close, production must not stop.

Supply-chain resilience and import-disruption mitigation for critical production systems — from pharmaceutical ingredients to medical devices to defensive hardware. Deployable manufacturing under stress.

  • • Supply-chain vulnerability mapping & alternative sourcing
  • • Scenario modelling: trade embargo, border closure, logistics breakdown
  • • Rapidly deployable containerised production for crisis response
V IT / AI

The brain of the system. Predictive, real-time, distributed.

The unifying technology layer across all five pillars — the agentize.eu Multi-Agent System and Agentic GraphRAG, coordinated by the AIR orchestration layer. One platform, multiple domains of protection. Not a dashboard. A coordination layer that acts.

  • • agentize.eu — operational today, 99.5% uptime SLA
  • • Agentic GraphRAG — context-aware cross-domain reasoning
  • • 12+ months ahead of hyperscaler multi-domain roadmaps

§ 02 — Integration Advantage

Separate pillars fail. Integrated systems endure.

PSR³ is — to our knowledge — the only framework that explicitly models the cross-dependencies between the domains that define modern critical infrastructure. In the real world these dependencies are not theoretical. They are existential.

Dependency · 01

Energy feeds everything.

Saudi Arabia alone consumes ~300,000 barrels of oil daily for desalination. A hospital without power is not a hospital. Energy failure cascades instantly into water, healthcare, and production failure.

Dependency · 02

Defence protects the chain.

Drone and missile threats do not target one pillar at a time — they target the whole network. Counter-drone and resilient data architecture protect energy, water, and healthcare simultaneously.

Dependency · 03

AI orchestrates it all.

One multi-agent platform, multiple domains of protection. The same AIR orchestration layer monitors grid anomalies, medical device fleets, and drone signatures — and coordinates response across them.

Single-agent AI systems fail the majority of the time in production environments. Critical infrastructure cannot afford that. PSR³ is delivered on agentize.eu — a multi-agent orchestration platform coordinated by the AIR layer.

§ 06 — Origin & Delivery

An open methodology, stewarded by practitioners.

PSR³ was developed by Valentin Nemeth — transformation strategist, OpenEXO partner, and faculty member teaching transformation governance at Széchenyi University. The framework draws on the Exponential Organization (ExO) methodology and three decades of large-scale institutional change across European energy, DACH, CEE, and GCC advisory engagements.

The framework is maintained as open methodology. It is delivered through an international consortium of 110+ specialists across 8 countries, coordinated by two brands: goexo.eu for strategic consulting and methodology licensing, and agentize.eu for AI multi-agent orchestration technology.

The methodology is also available through a structured certification programme — Associate, Practitioner, and Master — designed to build internal resilience expertise within sovereign and enterprise operators, rather than permanent consulting dependency.
